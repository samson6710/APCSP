Research at Webb Biology/Health

We are Webb Biology/Health, a research-intensive student team at Webb, with major areas of research covering the breadth of biology and health sciences, seeking innovative solutions to today's health challenges. Our key research areas:

PLGA (Poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid))

From Samson Shi's research paper “Promoting Faster Burn Recovery”: Aiding Tissue Healing Through Biopolymers": Burns are prevalent in society, and the impact of even mild burns can be significant for patients: long recovery cycles, risk of wound infection, and ongoing medication use. Even if the wound recovers, it may end up with visible scarring. Conventional treatments at this stage do provide the necessary care, but with advanced biomaterials, the risks during the recovery period can be reduced, and the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of recovery can be realized. Our project is to conduct further research on the biopolymers poly (lactic-co-glycolic acid) and hyaluronic acid (PLGA-HA), which have been approved for use by the FDA, and to improve the feasibility of combining them to aid in burn recovery. Both materials have been shown to aid in the recovery of soft tissue in the body. However, there are very few examples of combining the two and applying them to human recovery. Our project plans to utilize the strong biocompatibility and biodegradability of PLGA-HA to develop a bandage that protects wounds and accelerates the healing process of burn wounds, thus alleviating the pain of burn patients during recovery and shortening the duration of side effects caused by medications.

Neuroscience

From Samson Shi's research paper “Neuroimplants research”: Modern neuroimplants use advanced electroshock technologies and are, for instance, usually implanted in different parts of the brain to record neural activity and provoke neurons. For example: Brain-Machine Interfaces (BMI): Neural signals recordable from neuroimplants decode the neural control of users' electrical brain activity, thus producing a signal that activates an external device. Patients unable to move their limbs can control body exoskeletons, robotic arms, computer cursors, and so forth to restore limb movement or interaction, which is done with the help of Brain-Machine Interfaces. Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS): This type of neurostimulating neural implant comprises the placement of deep-brain stimulating electrodes, and then subsequently, electrical impulses are made in order to treat the symptoms of a neurological disorder, e.g., Parkinson's Disease and Depression. Neuronal activity elicited by electrical stimulation through implanted electrodes at special locations in the brain.

Drosophila

From Samson Shi's research paper Drosophila research”: Drosophila melanogaster has a short life cycle. The cycle consists of four stages: embryo, larva, pupa and adult. Since it has a short developmental cycle, it is ideal for genetic, biochemical and molecular analyses. From fertilized egg to adult pupation takes about 10 days at 25°C, with a maximum lifespan of 60 to 80 days under typical culture conditions. The life of this holometabolous insect begins as an embryo, progresses through the larval and pupal stages, and culminates in adulthood, making it possible to rapidly generate large populations for research.
